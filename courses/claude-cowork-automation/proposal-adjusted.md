## 01 Course Overview

Schools lose enormous staff time to work that is tedious but not technically complex: sorting files, compiling reports from scattered sources, drafting routine communications, and pulling data out of PDFs, forms, and receipts. Traditional automation asks staff to *build* the solution first — design a flow, configure an app, learn a tool — which is exactly the barrier most administrative and operations staff do not have time to clear.

Claude Cowork takes a different approach. It is Anthropic's agentic desktop "coworker" — generally available in 2026 and running on the Macs ISKL staff already use. Rather than building a flow, staff describe the task in plain language; Claude produces a step-by-step plan, waits for approval, and then executes it — reading and writing local files, using the browser and everyday applications, and connecting to tools such as Google Drive and Gmail so it works directly with the school's existing Google data. Scheduled tasks let it run recurring work — a morning email digest, a weekly report — automatically.

This 1-day programme is built for non-technical school staff. It is anchored on Cowork's permission-first design: every consequential action is reviewed before it runs, and access is scoped to only the folders and tools a task needs. Because Cowork is a rapidly evolving product whose output must always be verified, the programme trains habits of review rather than blind trust, and **all practical work is carried out on a sandbox dataset of synthetic data — never live student or staff records.** Participants leave able to delegate real ISKL work safely, with particular attention to data privacy, and to automate multi-step tasks without building a single flow or writing a line of code.

> **Delivery note:** Realised value depends on ISKL provisioning Claude licences (a recurring per-seat cost) and agreeing a data-handling policy before adoption. These prerequisites are listed in Section 03 and Section 10 and should be confirmed before the session is scheduled.

## 03 Entry Requirements

**Per participant**
- An ISKL-managed macOS computer with the Claude desktop app **pre-installed**
- A paid Claude licence (**Team or Enterprise recommended** for staff rollout, admin controls, and data governance) with Cowork enabled **before Day 1**
- Day-to-day familiarity with files, folders, and Google Workspace (Drive, Gmail)
- No programming experience required

**Pre-engagement (ISKL IT, before the session)**
- Pre-authorise the Google Drive and Gmail connectors for participant accounts (connector setup may require administrator consent)
- Provide a dedicated **sandbox** for labs: a test Google account or shared Drive folder populated with **synthetic sample data** — no live student or staff records
- Confirm an ISKL data-handling / AI-use policy covering autonomous-agent access

## 04 Course Objectives

At the end of this programme, participants will be able to:

1. Explain how Claude Cowork's agentic, plan-then-execute model differs from flow-builder and app-builder automation
2. Configure Claude Cowork's permissions on macOS, scope its file and folder access, and confirm its connection to Google Drive and Gmail
3. Delegate a multi-step task in plain language and review, adjust, or approve Claude's plan before it executes
4. Automate document preparation and the extraction of structured data from unstructured files such as PDFs and reports, verifying the output
5. Configure a scheduled task that runs recurring work automatically, accounting for the device and app availability it requires
6. Apply permission-first and data-privacy practices appropriate to handling student and staff information

## 05 Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of the programme, participants will be able to:

- Decide when to delegate a task to an AI agent versus build a traditional flow
- Configure Claude Cowork, scope its access safely, and confirm its connection to their Google data
- Write a clear task brief, steer Claude's plan, and supervise execution to completion
- Produce finished deliverables — sorted files, structured drafts, reports, and extracted datasets — and verify them before use
- Set up and monitor a recurring scheduled automation, understanding its device dependency
- Handle student and staff data responsibly when working with an autonomous agent

## 07 Course Outline

*1-day programme · 8 hours of intensive, hands-on learning*

**Hour 1 — A New Kind of Automation: Meet Claude Cowork**
- Three paradigms: flow builders, app builders, and agentic coworkers
- How Cowork works: describe → plan → approve → execute
- Where it runs: the desktop, local files, your apps, and the browser
- When to delegate to an agent versus build a flow

**Hour 2 — Setup Check, Permissions & Connecting Your Google Data**
- Verifying the pre-installed desktop app and signing in to the provisioned licence
- The permission-first model: granting and scoping folder access
- Confirming the Google Drive and Gmail connectors (pre-authorised by IT)
- Safety defaults: reviewing plans, approving actions, and stopping a run

**Hour 3 — Your First Delegated Task**
- Writing a clear task brief in plain language
- Reading Claude's step-by-step plan and steering it
- Approving, adjusting, or cancelling before anything happens
- Inspecting results and iterating

**Hour 4 — File & Inbox Automation**
- Sorting, renaming, and deduplicating a sample folder
- Triaging and summarising a sample inbox
- Filing attachments to the right Drive location
- Verifying before anything is moved or sent

**Hour 5 — Document Preparation & Data Extraction**
- Turning a pile of sample source files into a structured draft
- Extracting data from sample PDFs, forms, and reports into a spreadsheet
- Generating a report or presentation deliverable
- Quality and accuracy checks on agent output

**Hour 6 — Scheduled & Recurring Automations**
- Scheduled tasks: setting a cadence, and what the device and app must have running for them to fire
- A worked example: a morning digest or weekly report on sandbox data
- Awareness of long-running tasks and parallel sub-agents (demonstration, not a build)
- Monitoring scheduled runs, verifying output, and handling failures

**Hour 7 — Governance, Privacy & Safe Delegation for Schools**
- Handling student and staff data with an autonomous agent
- Scoping access narrowly: what Claude should and should not touch
- Reviewing consequential actions; accountability and audit
- Organisational rollout: Team and Enterprise controls and policy

**Hour 8 — Hands-On Lab: Automate a Real ISKL Task End-to-End**
- Choose a realistic task on the sandbox dataset: report compilation, communications drafting, data extraction, or file organisation
- Brief Claude, review the plan, and execute with oversight on sample data
- Add a scheduled run where it fits the workflow
- Present the automation and the privacy/oversight decisions made; trainer observation and feedback
